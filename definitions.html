Definitions and Acronyms

38 terms and acronyms used throughout the CoffCo Systems Engineering portfolio

Source: SRS V3.0 Introduction sheet Terms: 38 Scope: All portfolio documents
Scope. These definitions apply across all CoffCo Systems Engineering documents. Where a term has a specific technical meaning in this programme that differs from common usage, that meaning is stated here and takes precedence.
Term / AcronymDefinition
CONOPSConcept of Operations — Vacation Stables document defining the operational context and user needs
OPSCONOperational Concept — CoffCo document defining the system's intended operational behaviour (COFFCO-OC-001)
SRSSystem Requirements Specification — the requirements baseline document (COFFCO-SRS-001)
MVPMinimum Viable Product — the initial system to be demonstrated to investors
UserA hotel guest occupying a Vacation Stables room — the primary operator of the machine
OperatorVacation Stables housekeeping staff responsible for restocking and basic cleaning
AmericanoA concentrated coffee-based drink produced by diluting extracted coffee with hot water
BarUnit of pressure; 1 bar ≈ 100 kPa. Standard barista-quality coffee extraction pressure is approximately 1.1 bar
EU Energy Rating AHighest efficiency band under EU Ecodesign and Energy Labelling Regulations
EMCElectromagnetic Compatibility — the ability of the machine to operate without causing interference to other devices
IEC 60335International standard for safety of household and similar electrical appliances
HACCPHazard Analysis and Critical Control Points — food safety management standard
GDPRGeneral Data Protection Regulation — EU data protection legislation
MTBFMean Time Between Failures — a measure of system reliability
MTTRMean Time To Repair — a measure of maintainability
ALARPAs Low As Reasonably Practicable — safety risk reduction principle
BERBeyond Economical Repair — threshold at which assembly replacement is preferred over repair
COTSCommercial Off-The-Shelf — standard commercially available component
EARSEasy Approach to Requirements Syntax — structured requirements notation
FMECAFailure Modes, Effects, and Criticality Analysis
FSMFinite State Machine — a model of system behaviour through discrete states
ILSIntegrated Logistics Support — structured approach to through-life product support
IPSIntegrated Product Support — the complete support plan for the system lifecycle
LRULine Replaceable Unit — an assembly designed for rapid field replacement
MCUMicrocontroller Unit — the main processor on the Control Assembly PCBA
OTAOver-the-Air — wireless firmware or software update mechanism
PCBAPrinted Circuit Board Assembly
PRVPressure Relief Valve — safety device limiting maximum system pressure
RCMReliability-Centred Maintenance — methodology for optimising maintenance strategy
RPNRisk Priority Number — FMECA severity × occurrence × detectability score
SDASystems Design Authority — the technical authority for the CoffCo programme
SOSESchool of Systems Engineering — academic programme context for this portfolio
SSMSoft Systems Methodology — Checkland (1981) systems thinking framework
TCOThermal Cutoff — non-resettable hardware overtemperature safety device
TLSThrough Life Support — the Design for X theme governing this programme
VCRMVerification and Compliance Requirements Matrix — maps requirements to V&V evidence
WEEEWaste Electrical and Electronic Equipment — EU/UK recycling regulation
WDTWatchdog Timer — hardware circuit that resets the MCU if firmware heartbeat is lost
Verification — TestPhysical testing of the system to confirm a requirement is met
Verification — DemonstrationObservation of the system performing a function to confirm it is met
Verification — AnalysisReview of data, calculations, or models to confirm a requirement is met
Verification — InspectionVisual or physical examination to confirm a requirement is met
