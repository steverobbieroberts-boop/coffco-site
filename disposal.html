Design for Disposal. The zero-landfill target (SRS 3.7) and minimum 50% recycled content requirement (SRS 3.4) are embedded in the requirements — not added at the end. The modular architecture designed for maintainability directly enables selective component recovery. Disposal dividends were designed in from the start.
1
LRU and Mid-Life Upgrades
Modular replacement and technology refresh across the 5–7 year service life
1.1 Line Replaceable Units

The assembly-swap maintenance model means all three primary assemblies are already LRUs. Each is removed and replaced by a field engineer in under 4 hours. At end of assembly life the unit returns to depot — refurbished if viable, disposed of if beyond economical repair (BER).

Control Assembly
PCB, firmware, wireless module, NTC sensor. Target life: 5–7 years. Refurbishment: component replacement at depot. BER trigger: corrosion, physical damage, or obsolete firmware platform with no upgrade path.
Thermal Assembly
Heating element, pump, PRV, seals, check valve, frother. Target life: 5–7 years (seals replaced annually at PM-03). BER trigger: heater tube wall failure, pump casing craze, or irreparable scale damage.
Power Supply Unit
Universal switch-mode module, plug modules. Target life: 5–7 years (electrical integrity check at PM-07 Year 5). BER trigger: insulation resistance below 1 MΩ or failed IEC 60335 retest.
Plug modules
Regional variants (UK/EU/US). Treated as consumables — replaced on damage or regulatory change. No refurbishment; direct to recycling.
Fluid path seals
FKM O-rings and gaskets. Consumable. Annual scheduled replacement at PM-03. No recovery value — waste stream.
1.2 Mid-Life Upgrades

The 5–7 year service life spans multiple technology generations. Mid-life upgrades address technology obsolescence without full machine replacement — consistent with Design for Longevity.

Wireless module
TD-06 deferred. Module is a discrete COTS component — replacement at mid-life (Year 3–4) allows technology refresh without Control Assembly swap. Old module to certified e-waste recycler.
Firmware platform
Delivered via OTA update (SRS 1.11) wherever possible — no hardware change. If RTOS becomes unsupported, Control Assembly swap carries the upgraded platform.
NTC holder (OA-IPS-01)
PP to PPS plastic upgrade. Rolled into next Control Assembly refurbishment batch — no machine downtime. PPS is more recyclable and more stable for recycler processing.
Regional plug adapter
Plug standard changes (e.g. BS 1363 revision) isolated to a single low-cost component by the interchangeable plug module design (TD-03). PSU unaffected.
Peltier cooler
Thermoelectric cooler technology improving rapidly. Mid-life swap to higher-efficiency module reduces energy consumption and supports Vacation Stables 2030 sustainability target.
2
Environmental Considerations
Minimising environmental impact across the disposal process
Zero-landfill
SRS 3.7 mandates confirmed recycling, reuse, or recovery routes for all waste streams. No CoffCo waste goes to landfill — machine assemblies, consumable packaging, fluid path residues, descale waste.
Circular economy
SRS 3.4 (≥50% recycled content) means the casing is already partly recycled polymer. End-of-life recycling closes the loop — shredded polymer re-enters the recycled polymer supply chain as secondary raw material.
Electronic waste
PCBAs contain precious metals (gold, silver, palladium on contacts and PCB pads). All PCBAs to certified WEEE-compliant e-waste recycler for precious metal recovery — positive residual value, partially offsetting disposal cost.
Peltier cooler
No F-gases or refrigerant fluid — solid-state thermoelectric device. No refrigerant disposal obligations. A direct advantage of Peltier over compressor-based milk refrigeration.
Fluid residues
Thermal Assembly fluid path: water residues and scale deposits. HACCP clean performed before dispatch. Citric acid-based descale fluid is non-hazardous but flushed to drain, not landfill.
Packaging
All packaging either recyclable or returnable. No single-use plastic film. EPP foam inserts: recyclable. Corrugated cardboard carton: recyclable. Return carton scheme in place.
3
Resource Management
Maximising material recovery and resource efficiency at end of life

The modular architecture enables refurbishment-first as a governing principle — BER declaration is a last resort, not a default. SRS 3.7 mandates that all waste streams have a confirmed recycling, reuse, or recovery route, making landfill prohibited by requirement. The target recovery rate across all material streams is ≥99%. The material recovery hierarchy governs decision-making:

1
Reuse
Whole assembly refurbishment and redeployment — highest value, preferred outcome
2
Remanufacture
Component-level repair at depot
3
Recycle
Material recovery from BER assemblies — PCBA precious metals, PPS polymer, metals
4
Energy recovery
Where material recycling is not viable
✕
Landfill
Prohibited — zero-landfill target (SRS 3.7)
Precious metal recovery
Control Assembly PCBAs yield approximately 0.1–0.3 g of gold per board. At scale, PCBA recycling generates positive recovery value. All PCBAs to London Metal Exchange-registered recycler with documented recovery rates.
PPS polymer recovery
PPS casing and recycled polymer body: material-separated before processing. PPS has high intrinsic value as a recycled polymer — routed to specialist PPS recycler rather than general plastics stream.
Metal recovery
Stainless steel (thermal pathway), copper (wiring), aluminium (heater tube, chassis reinforcement): separated by material type at disassembly. All to certified metal recycler.
Data sanitisation
Control Assembly flash memory holds machine config and fault log — no guest personal data (GDPR). Cloud platform deregisters machine on collection; local flash overwritten to zero. No physical PCBA destruction required.
4
Health and Safety
Worker and community protection throughout disposal activities
COSHH compliance
Descale fluid (citric acid — irritant, GHS07), solder flux residues (low hazard but ventilation required), cleaning agents. COSHH assessment completed before disposal activities commence. Appropriate PPE specified.
Electrical safety
All machines fully de-energised before collection. Field engineer confirms mains cable removed. Class II construction means no accessible live voltage in de-energised state.
Thermal hazard
Thermal Assembly may retain heat if recently operated. Minimum 30-minute cool-down required. Field engineer uses calibrated thermocouple to confirm surface temperature below 40°C before packaging.
Manual handling
Machine maximum 10 kg (SRS 4.10) — within single-person lift guidelines for a trained operative. Thermal Assembly maximum 4 kg. No mechanical handling aids required for routine collection.
Food safety (HACCP)
Thermal Assembly contains food-contact surfaces. HACCP clean before dispatch. Disposable food-grade gloves worn during fluid path handling. Assembly sealed in food-grade bag for transit.
Worker training
All depot staff and field engineers: COSHH awareness; manual handling; electrical safety (IEC 60335); HACCP food safety certificate; CoffCo disposal SOP familiarisation. Training records maintained by CoffCo HR.
5
Regulatory Compliance
Legislative requirements governing disposal in UK, EU, and US markets
WEEE (UK/EU)
CoffCo is the producer and holds the WEEE take-back obligation. Registered with approved Producer Compliance Scheme (PCS). WEEE registration number displayed on product and recorded in cloud platform.
RoHS 3
All restricted hazardous substances (lead, mercury, cadmium, hexavalent chromium, PBB, PBDE, DEHP, BBP, DBP, DIBP) confirmed absent from design. RoHS compliance declaration held and supplied to recycler.
REACH
SVHCs identified and disclosed per REACH Article 33. Recycler notified of SVHC content. No SVHC above 0.1% w/w threshold permitted in product (design constraint).
Basel Convention
If assemblies exported internationally for recycling, Basel prior informed consent (PIC) procedure applies for hazardous waste. CoffCo compliance team confirms classification before cross-border shipment.
IEC 60335-1
Type-test certificate and CE/UKCA/UL Declaration of Conformity archived for minimum 10 years from end of production. Required for WEEE compliance and product liability. Machine-level certificate mapping in cloud platform.
US market
State-level e-waste legislation varies (California SB 20, New York E-Waste law). US-deployed machines returned to US-based certified recycler or shipped back under Basel notification. UL/ETL documentation retained 10 years.
Documentation retention
All disposal records retained minimum 10 years: WEEE transfer notes, hazardous waste consignment notes, recycler certificates of destruction, cloud platform disposal events. Accessible to regulators on request.
6
Stakeholder and Supplier Engagement
Roles and responsibilities across the disposal supply chain
CoffCo
Primary disposal authority. Owns all WEEE take-back obligations. Operates machine collection logistics. Manages regional depot disassembly and assessment. Contracts certified recyclers. Archives all compliance documentation.
Vacation Stables
Notifies CoffCo of machines reaching end of contract. Ensures machine is de-energised and accessible for collection. Routes consumable waste through CoffCo recycling scheme via housekeeping SOP.
CoffCo field engineer
Collects machine from hotel. Completes disposal collection record in cloud platform (serial number, condition, date). Performs de-energisation and cool-down verification. Packages in return carton for depot transport.
CoffCo regional depot
Receives returned machines. Performs condition assessment: refurbish or BER. Disassembles BER machines to component level. Segregates material streams. Manages COSHH and HACCP during disassembly.
E-waste recycler
Handles PCBAs, Control Assembly, PSU electronics. Must hold: EA waste carrier licence; ISO 14001 certification; BS 8887-2 or equivalent. Provides certificates of destruction and recovery rate documentation. Annual audit by CoffCo procurement.
Consumable supply partner
Contractually required to operate a take-back scheme (SRS 3.7 condition of supply). Operates collection logistics and demonstrates recycling route for coffee grounds and packaging. Annual sustainability report to CoffCo.
7
Long-Term Considerations
Post-disposal obligations and future-proofing the disposal strategy
Regulatory evolution
WEEE, RoHS, REACH, and EPR regulations evolve continuously. Disposal plan reviewed annually against current regulatory baseline. If regulations change mid-product-life, plan updated and communicated to depot and recyclers within 90 days.
Obsolescence management
When COTS components (pump, PRV, NTC holder, WDT IC) become obsolete, last-time-buy (LTB) triggered. LTB quantity = remaining fleet size × annual failure rate × remaining service life. Held at depot until fleet decommissioned.
Cloud platform exit
If CoffCo cloud platform is retired, all machine records exported to PDF/A archive before shutdown. Contractual data retention clause with cloud provider. Records must survive corporate events (merger, acquisition).
Gen 2 transition
When CoffCo Gen 2 launches, Gen 1 machines enter accelerated disposal/upgrade programme. Compatible Gen 1 machines may receive Control Assembly upgrade to Gen 2 platform. Disposal programme planned 12 months before Gen 2 commercial launch.
CSR reporting
Annual disposal sustainability metrics in CoffCo ESG report: machines collected, recovery rate (target ≥99% of material by mass), materials recovered by weight, consumable waste diverted from landfill, disposal logistics carbon emissions. Feeds into Vacation Stables ESG score.

SRS requirements written with disposal in mind — disposal outcomes are the result of design decisions, not late-stage planning
SRS Ref Requirement Disposal benefit
SRS 3.4Minimum 50% recycled content in machine casingRecycled polymer casing re-enters the recycled polymer supply chain at end of life — circular loop closed. Reduces virgin material demand.
SRS 3.7All waste products from operation shall have a recycling or reuse routeZero-landfill target embedded in requirements before design began. Forces confirmed recycling routes before deployment.
SRS 4.8IEC 60335-1 complianceType-test certificate required for WEEE compliance. Ensures machine is correctly classified and registered.
SRS 4.9Food safety — HACCP complianceFood-grade materials documented. Simplifies disposal — no unknown food-contact contaminants to declare.
SP-02Modular physical architectureEnables selective assembly recovery. BER Control Assembly does not require disposal of a serviceable Thermal Assembly. Maximises refurbishment rate.
TD-05Anti-theft cable lug — tool-free removalDesigned for tool-free removal at end of life. Removes a disassembly complexity that would otherwise slow collection.
TD-03Interchangeable plug moduleRegional regulatory change requires only plug module disposal/replacement — PSU is retained. Isolates regional risk to a single low-cost component.
OA-IPS-01NTC holder upgrade PP → PPSPPS is more recyclable and more stable than PP. Upgrade removes a lower-grade polymer from the waste stream.
