2.4 Firmware Management

  • Firmware version controlled in CoffCo Git repository. Each deployed machine has a current firmware version recorded in the cloud platform asset register.
  • OTA updates pushed by cloud ops during low-usage window (03:00–06:00 local time). Machine must be in STANDBY state. Update process: download → signature verify → install → reboot → confirm STANDBY. Rollback triggered automatically if machine does not reach STANDBY within 120 s post-update.
  • Manual firmware update via diagnostic tablet used when machine has no Wi-Fi access (fallback for TD-06 failure or hotel IT issues).
  • Firmware change notes captured in Technical Publications CMS and linked to relevant SRS requirement. If update changes an operating procedure, associated online manual updated before firmware is deployed.

4.2 Handling

  • Thermal Assembly: maximum mass 4 kg. One-person lift within manual handling guidelines (HSE Manual Handling Operations Regulations 1992). No mechanical aids required for routine assembly swap.
  • Full machine (new installation): maximum mass 10 kg (SRS 4.10). One-person carry acceptable for flat-surface hotel room delivery. Use of sack truck for multi-floor hotel deliveries where lift access is unavailable.
  • ESD precautions: anti-static wrist strap mandatory when handling Control Assembly PCB. ESD bag used during transit.
  • HACCP handling: Thermal Assembly handled with food-grade disposable gloves when any fluid path surfaces are accessible. HACCP cleaning completed before assembly is returned to storage.

8.2 Human Factors — Field Engineer and Maintenance Team

Human Factors ElementCoffCo Approach
Ergonomics — assembly swapThermal Assembly maximum 4 kg one-person lift. All panels accessed from front or top — no under-machine access required. Anti-tamper screws located at accessible heights. No kneeling or sustained awkward postures for routine PM-03.
Ergonomics — hotel room workingMachine located on hotel desk at standing/seated working height. Field engineer does not need to move furniture for routine service. Lighting: standard hotel room lighting is adequate; portable lamp carried for poorly lit hotel rooms.
Safety — electrical isolationMains power isolated at hotel room socket before any chassis access. Fuse panel accessible to field engineer only (SRS 3.11). IEC 60335 awareness briefing in FE qualification. Lock-out procedure: unplug and confirm no power before removing any panel.
Safety — thermal hazardField engineer does not work on live thermal assembly under operating temperature. Cool-down period: 30 minutes after power-off before Thermal Assembly access. Calibrated thermocouple confirms surface temperature ≤48°C (SRS 3.10) before any surface contact.
Safety — HACCP handlingFood-grade disposable gloves for fluid path contact. Sealed HACCP bag for Thermal Assembly transit. Food-grade cleaning agent confirmed non-reactive with skin. COSHH assessment for descale fluid completed by CoffCo and shared with field engineers.
Error prevention — FIDA formatFIDA maintenance procedures are structured to prevent action before diagnosis. Field engineer cannot reach Action step without completing Diagnosis. Tier boundary clearly stated in each FIDA entry: 'VS maintenance team — stop here; escalate to CoffCo field engineer'.
Error prevention — diagnostic tabletDiagnostic tablet guides field engineer step-by-step through assembly swap with visual reference. Assembly is keyed (mechanical) to prevent installation in wrong orientation. Firmware re-registration is mandatory step on Control Assembly swap — tablet prompts before closing work order.

8.3 Recruitment and Retention

  • Field engineers are the critical resource for CoffCo IPS delivery. Target profile: HNC/HND in electrical or mechanical engineering, or equivalent experience. Electrical bias preferred (IEC 60335 electrical safety work). HACCP food handler certificate desirable.
  • Career framework: Junior FE (0–2 years, supervised) → FE (2+ years, independent) → Senior FE (5+ years, Tier 3 escalation, training delivery). Senior FEs can progress to CoffCo engineering team.
  • Retention: competitive day rates for contracted FEs; mileage and accommodation covered for travel-heavy regions; diagnostic tablet and tooling provided (not personal equipment). Annual refresher training maintains engagement and skills currency.
  • Cloud ops analysts: graduate-level IT/software operations profile. Shift-premium pay for out-of-hours coverage. Progression into CoffCo platform engineering team.

10.1 Design for Supportability

  • Modular physical architecture (SP-02): assembly-swap model achieves MTTR ≤4 h without requiring component-level repair in the field. Each physical element is independently replaceable.
  • Tool-free housekeeping access (SRS 2.5): water reservoir, drip tray, and consumable chamber all accessible without tools. Tier 1 maintenance requires zero specialist equipment.
  • Accessibility: all Tier 2 serviceable panels accessed from front or top. No machine movement required for any routine maintenance task.
  • Life cycle cost considerations: assembly-swap model has higher upfront spares cost but lower MTTR and lower field engineer skill requirement than component-level repair. LCC modelling confirms swap model is preferred for a hotel room deployment context where minimising downtime is the primary commercial driver.
