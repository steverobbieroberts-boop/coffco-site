Safety-critical failure modes — Category A (Hidden). FM-07 (dry-fire interlock), FM-09 (PSU insulation), FM-13 (sensor monitoring), FM-14 (telemetry), FM-15 (interlock logic), FM-17 (status display), FM-24 (NTC holder perforation): all require mandatory annual failure-finding tasks. These tasks cannot be deferred or omitted without formal SDA waiver and documented risk acceptance.
Issue 1.1 update — Fan et al. (2009) gap analysis. Three failure modes absent from Issue 1.0 have been added following comparison with Fan Jinjuan et al. (2009) Engineering Failure Analysis, as cited in the IPS Plan §10.4. FM-23 (pump debris clogging — ranked #1 failure mode in Fan et al.) · FM-24 (NTC holder perforation — PP material fails at ~100°C, Cat A hidden safety) · FM-25 (fluid path seal degradation — incorrect elastomer root cause). See IPS open actions OA-IPS-01, OA-IPS-02, OA-IPS-03.

RCM2 Consequence Categories
The category determines the logical order of task selection — safety before operational before economic
Cat Category Definition
ASafety/Environmental — HiddenFailure mode is hidden from operating crew AND could cause injury, death, or environmental harm. Mandatory failure-finding task required. Cannot be deferred.
BSafety/Environmental — EvidentFailure mode is evident to operating crew AND could cause injury, death, or environmental harm. On-condition or scheduled task required.
COperational — EvidentFailure has direct adverse effect on operational capability (availability, output, guest service). Economic justification for preventive task.
DNon-Operational — EvidentFailure has no direct effect on safety or operations. Preventive task justified only if cheaper than reactive repair.
