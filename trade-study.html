Study conclusion. Smart/IoT machine type ranks first (163/240, 67.9%) driven by the highest ConOps fit score (8/10) reflecting IoT telemetry, remote maintenance, and guest profiling alignment with the Vacation Stables design brief. Single-serve ranks second (160/240, 66.7%) with strong safety and footprint scores. The CoffCo MVP draws from both — Smart/IoT architecture with single-serve simplicity. Note: this study is implementation-neutral; no consumable format (capsule, pod, bean) is specified.

Evaluation Criteria and Weights
7 criteria · maximum 240 points · ★ = criteria added in v2 or v4
ConOps fit ×5max 50
Operational model alignment — primary design driver. Unsupervised hotel room deployment without guest training.
Safety ×4max 40
Unsupervised hotel room deployment safety. Hot liquids, electrical safety, no technical support on-site.
Bev. quality ★ ×5max 50
Café-quality barista output including milk drinks — core value proposition. Added v2.
Footprint ×3max 30
300×300×300mm hard constraint (SRS 3.2). Score based on maximum dimension.
8-yr TCO ★ ×4max 40
8-year total cost of ownership per room at 2 brews/day, £0.25/kWh. Added v4 — replaces individual cost/maintenance/power scores.
Noise ★ ×2max 20
Hotel room early-morning context. Guest experience impact. Added v2.
Weight ×1max 10
Deployment and chassis loading. Lowest priority criterion.
