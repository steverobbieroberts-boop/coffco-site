SS-2 + SS-5
Control and Monitoring Assembly
The brain of the CoffCo machine — integrates brew control, safety firmware functions, wireless telemetry, and non-volatile fault logging into a single PCBA assembly. SS-2 (brew control) and SS-5 (monitoring and comms) were merged into a single control assembly at V2.2 to reduce the internal interface count from 3 to 2, eliminating one class of failure modes (TD-04).

SS-2 / SS-3
Thermal Assembly
The primary SWAP (Size, Weight, and Power) driver. Unified thermal assembly with fluid switching eliminates a separate milk thermal path, reducing component count and seal failure surface area by 40% vs a dual-path architecture (TD-02). Fan et al. (2009) identified scale accumulation as the #1 failure driver — the descale scheduling and PRV design directly address this.

Key components and design characteristics
ComponentFunction and design characteristicSRS
Heating elementCOTSStandard resistive element. Heats water to 60–90°C within ≤180 s brew cycle. COTS commodity — meets performance requirement with adequate margin. Bespoke induction heating offers faster response but at materially higher MVP cost.4.1, 4.11
Bi-metallic TCOCOTSNon-resettable hardware thermal cutoff opens at ≥100°C independently of firmware. Safety-critical — provides hardware-layer protection independent of the MCU and firmware watchdog. Three-layer overtemperature protection: NTC firmware → thermostat → TCO.3.10
Pump (≤1.3 bar)COTSSmall-volume COTS pump. Provides extraction pressure 0.9–1.3 bar. Fan et al.: pump clogging from scale is the #1 failure component — descale scheduling (SRS 1.6) is the primary preventive control. Pump strainer specified (OA-IPS-02).1.5, 4.4, 4.5
Pressure Relief ValveCOTSMechanical PRV ≤2.5 bar; hardware-only; vents exclusively into drip tray (not inside chassis). Fan et al. identified PRV venting inside chassis as a hidden design hazard — this design eliminates that failure mode by routing vent to drip tray. Annual PM-05 functional test.3.13
Milk refrigeration (Peltier)COTSCompact thermoelectric cooler. Maintains milk store ≤4°C. Compressor-based refrigeration is oversized and heavier for the milk volume involved. Continuous power draw — largest single impact on EU energy efficiency target.1.2, 4.2, 4.3
Fluid pathwayBespokeCoffCo-designed thermoplastic channel. All fluid paths physically isolated from electrical conductors by ≥6 mm. Food-grade material throughout. FKM seals — annual replacement PM-03 (OA-IPS-03).3.11, 4.9
Frother / fluid switchBespokeMilk aeration and coffee/milk path switching. Automated flush cycle between drink types prevents cross-contamination (SRS 4.9, FM-04). Bespoke design — no suitable COTS switching mechanism available at required performance and footprint.1.2, 4.9
SS-4
Power Supply Unit
Fully COTS. A certified universal switch-mode PSU module eliminates the cost and schedule risk of independent IEC 60335 and EMC certification at MVP scale. Class II double insulation enables a standardised 2-pin plug across all three markets, removing earth-path dependency and supporting the interchangeable plug module strategy (TD-03).

Key design characteristics
CharacteristicDesign detailSRS
Universal AC input100–240 V, 50/60 Hz switch-mode topology. Single PSU variant for UK, EU, and US markets. No hardware change between markets — only the interchangeable plug module changes.2.3, 2.4
IEC 60335-1 Class IIDouble insulation — no accessible surface presents live voltage under any single fault. Enables 2-pin plug across all markets. No earth-path dependency eliminates field failure risk from missing earth in hotel sockets.3.11, 4.8
Mains input fuseT-type slow-blow fuse at inlet. Hardware overcurrent protection independent of PSU internal protection. IEC 60335-1 Cl.25 compliance.3.11

SS-1
User Interface Panel
Exactly 3 controls — drink type, volume, brew confirmation. This is deliberate: minimising the control surface minimises the error surface for first-time unaided use (SRS 2.1). The two-step brew confirmation (BREW → CONFIRM BREW) satisfies H-19 child misuse prevention. Icon-only status ring eliminates localisation requirements for multi-language deployment.
